FACEIT Cooldown FAQ & Rules Guide

How does the cooldown escalation system work?

FACEIT tracks "Leaver", "noShow", and "AFK/Declined" infractions under a single, unified account index. Cooldowns are automated and stack. Every infraction you commit stays in your ban history for a rolling 30-day period. If you receive multiple bans in a 30-day window, your next ban will move up the escalation ladder, resulting in significantly longer restrictions (up to 7 days).

What is the difference between Leaver, noShow, and AFK?

  • Leaver: Applied if you are disconnected from a live server for a cumulative total of more than 5 minutes. Even if you reconnect and play the match, you will still receive the queue ban.
  • noShow: Applied if you accept the matchroom queue but fail to join the server during the designated warmup period (3 minutes for matchmaking, 10 minutes for tournaments).
  • AFK / Declined: Applied if you are in the queue, a match is found, but you fail to click the "Accept" button before the timer expires.

What is the Elo penalty for a Leaver?

If you get flagged with a "Leaver" penalty during a match, your Elo calculation for that match is heavily penalized:

  • If your team **loses**: You lose **double the Elo** (usually -50 Elo instead of -25).
  • If your team **wins**: You only receive **50% of the Elo** gain (usually +12 Elo instead of +25).
Additionally, these matches are hidden from your public match history.

How to generate a free FACEIT API Key

To enable automatic CS2 statistics tracking without manual entry, you can host your own API key:

  1. Visit the official FACEIT Developer Portal and log in with your FACEIT credentials.
  2. Navigate to App Studio in the top menu.
  3. Click Create an App. Fill in any name and details (e.g. "MyStats").
  4. Once the app is created, go to the API Keys tab.
  5. Create a new **Client** or **Server** API Key and copy it.
  6. Open app.js on your machine, find const DEFAULT_API_KEY on line 2, and paste your key inside the quotation marks. Once committed and pushed to GitHub, the tracker will run automatically for you and anyone else who searches a nickname!
